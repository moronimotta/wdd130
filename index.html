Santa Catarina, Brazil

Santa Catarina is a state located in the southern region of Brazil. It has numerous beaches and mountainous tourist spots. The majority of the population born in the state are descendants of Italians and Germans, and in some older locations, these languages are still spoken.

The capital of the state is Florianopolis, with its beautiful beaches and incredible cultural heritage. Despite being the capital, it is not the city with the highest population.

The state is also famous for its natural beauty in the interior, such as the Serra Catarinense, with its mountain landscapes, valleys, and waterfalls, and the Vale Europeu region, with its charming cities and European colonization.

The economy of Santa Catarina is diversified, with a focus on the textile, ceramic, automotive, metallurgical, and tourism industries. The state is known for its strong industrial base and entrepreneurship, being one of the most developed states in Brazil.

In Joinville, my hometown, the first member of the Church in Brazil was baptized in 1929, and the Church was first organized on October 25, 1931. The chapel still exists today (it's my ward).
